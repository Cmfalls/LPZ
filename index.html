Lead Animal Keeper - McCormick Bird House

I cared for cranes at ICF.
I want to bring that to McCormick.

I am a conservation biologist and avian care candidate whose work connects hands-on husbandry, behavioral observation, ecology, anthropology, and public-facing science communication.

For Lincoln Park Zoo's McCormick Bird House, I bring AZA-accredited aviculture experience, careful behavioral documentation, research discipline, and a communication style built for teams that need clear notes, reliable follow-through, and shared standards of care.

About Camryn

ANIMAL CARE, BEHAVIORAL OBSERVATION, AND CLEAR TEAM COMMUNICATION.

I am a University of Denver graduate in Anthropology and Ecology & Biodiversity (3.76 GPA, Dean's List). Before DU, I completed coursework at Bard College (3.93 GPA, Honors List). My avian care foundation comes from six months as an Aviculture Apprentice at the International Crane Foundation, working in an AZA-accredited setting with all 15 crane species through husbandry, behavioral observation, captive propagation, and crane handling.

Since returning to university, I have worked in three research labs at DU — evolutionary behavioral adaptation (Tinghitella Lab), conservation biology (Sher Lab), and evolutionary genetics (Larson Lab) — while building a portfolio of bird-focused research, writing, photography, and science communication.

The McCormick Bird House role asks for daily care, team communication, enrichment, records, and welfare awareness. Those are the threads I want this portfolio to make visible.

My strongest fit is the combination: hands-on aviculture, careful observation, comfort with documentation, and the ability to explain animal care and conservation clearly to both colleagues and the public.
